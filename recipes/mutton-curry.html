Mutton Curry

Description

What's a biryani without some succulent pieces of mutton wrapped in a perfect blend of aromatic spices and herbs!? Yes, though other meats being more common, biryani eaters all over the world can easily swear by mutton. And not just biryanis, curries, bakes and all other preparations with this wonderful meat are always special. Did you know, unlike other dishes, a day-old mutton prep will always be all the more tastier on the palate! Try it the next time you plan on to take on this meat.

Ingredients

  • Mutton
  • Oil
  • Red chilli powder
  • Salt
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Garam masala
  • Green cardamoms
  • Yogurt
  • Turmeric
  • Coriander

Steps

  1. Heat 2-3 tablespoons oil in a deep non-stick pan.
  2. Take mutton pieces in a bowl, add turmeric powder, chilli powder, salt, coriander powder, garam masala powder, coriander leaves and ginger-garlic paste and mix well.
  3. Add cinnamon, cloves and green cardamoms to hot oil in the pan and sauté till fragrant. Add onions and sauté till golden brown.
  4. Add marinated mutton pieces, mix well and saute for 4-5 minutes.
  5. Heat remaining oil in a pressure cooker. Add potatoes and salt and sauté for 1-2 minutes. Add mutton mixture, mix and saute for 1-2 minutes more.
  6. Add yogurt and mix. Adjust salt, mix, cover, reduce heat and pressure cook on medium heat till 4 whistles are released. Open the lid when the pressure settles completely.
  7. Transfer into a serving bowl, garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.
